What is QR Code Generator?

QR Code Generator is a free online tool that creates QR (Quick Response) codes from any text, URL, email address, phone number, or other data. QR codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can be scanned by smartphones and other devices to quickly access information. Originally invented in 1994 by Denso Wave for tracking automotive parts, QR codes have become ubiquitous in marketing, payments, authentication, and everyday information sharing.

Our QR Code Generator runs entirely in your browser, which means your data never leaves your device. There are no server uploads, no tracking, and no watermarks on the generated codes. You can customize the size and colors of your QR codes to match your branding or design requirements, and then download the result as a high-quality PNG image ready for print or digital use.

Whether you need a QR code for a business card, a restaurant menu, a product label, a Wi-Fi network, or a marketing flyer, this tool makes it easy to create professional QR codes in seconds without any software installation or account registration.

How to Use

  1. Enter Your Data: Type or paste the text, URL, email address, or any content you want to encode into the input field.
  2. Customize Appearance: Select your preferred QR code size from the dropdown menu (200x200 to 500x500 pixels). Choose custom foreground and background colors using the color pickers to match your branding.

  4. Test the Code: Use your smartphone camera or a QR scanner app to scan the generated code and verify it works correctly before distributing it.
  5. Download: Click the "Download PNG" button to save the QR code as a high-resolution PNG image file to your device.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of data can I encode in a QR code?

You can encode virtually any text data including website URLs, plain text, email addresses (mailto: links), phone numbers (tel: links), SMS messages, Wi-Fi network credentials, vCard contact information, and geographic coordinates. The most common use case is encoding URLs for quick access to websites.

Is there a limit to how much data a QR code can hold?

QR codes can store up to approximately 4,296 alphanumeric characters or 7,089 numeric characters. However, the more data you encode, the denser and more complex the QR code becomes, which can make it harder to scan. For best results, keep your content concise.

Will my QR codes expire?

No. QR codes generated by this tool are static, meaning the data is embedded directly in the code pattern. They will never expire or stop working. However, if you encode a URL that later becomes unavailable, the QR code will still work but the destination will be unreachable.

Can I use custom colors for my QR code?

Yes. You can choose any foreground and background color combination using the color pickers. Just make sure there is enough contrast between the two colors for scanners to read the code reliably. Dark foreground on light background works best.

Tips

  • Maintain high contrast: Always use a dark foreground color on a light background. Low contrast makes QR codes difficult or impossible for scanners to read.
  • Use the right size: For print materials, choose a larger size (400x400 or 500x500). For digital screens, 200x200 or 300x300 is usually sufficient.
  • Test before printing: Always scan your QR code with multiple devices before printing it on physical materials to ensure it works reliably.
  • Keep URLs short: Use URL shorteners for very long web addresses. Shorter data produces simpler QR codes that are easier and faster to scan.
